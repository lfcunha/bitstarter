NYCBUS is a mobile/web application for tracking New York City Transit Buses. The application provides a simple interface to locate the nearest bus to the user, requiring minimal interaction and input. New Yorkers are busy people. One thing we're know for is, we're always in a rush. What is the one thing guaranteed in this city? the buses never run on schedule! Don't wait at the bus stop wondering when is the next bus going to arrive. This application allows you to visually locate on a map the exact location of the bus. In addition, it will display live tracking of its progress.

Do you have something better to do than waiting at the bus stop? Set up an alert! Let the app at what time you'd like to take the bus and how long does it take you to get to the bus stop. Our service will call you when the nearest bus is much time away from arrival at the specified stop.

NYCBUS provides a extremely simple, yet powerful and feature rich, interface. The map centers at the user's location, and displays all bus lines within a short walking distance. The location of the nearests buses is immediately conveying in the map. Alternatively, the user may choose to turn on/off only specfic routes, set favorites, or search for bus lines.

Full List of Features:

Automatically display bus routes, with live bus position, within walking distance
Choose spific route to display
Set/display favorite routes
Pin map at a distant location
Search and display specific routes
Turn on traffic info and subway lines

FAQ

When will this app be available?
The app is currently available as a mobile web service. Iphone and Android versions will be submited to the respective stores in the fall of 2013. Notice that currently not all bus lines are GPS equiped. See website for more information. MTA estimates full fleet GPS equipped by the Spring of 2014.
How much will it cost?
The basic app is free to use. Premium paid services will be optional. One such service allows the user to request a phone call giving advance notice of bus arrival at a specified stop. This should be useful in scenarios such as late at night when buses are scarse and the user might be busy with work or other activities and would like to be notified, within a certain time frame, of when the next bus will arrive. All data is provided by the MTA, which is responsible of its accuracy.
